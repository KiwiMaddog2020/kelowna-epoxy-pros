# Brand Strategy

## Working Brand

`Kelowna Epoxy Pros` is the working brand from the supplied logo. Confirm whether this is the final public name before launch.

## Positioning

Kelowna homeowners and small business owners need floors that look sharp, clean easily, and can stand up to normal Okanagan life. The logo already has a bold contractor look, so the website should balance that with calm, clear, family-business confidence. Professional enough for commercial work, approachable enough for a family garage.

## Brand Promise

Proper prep, clear quotes, clean job sites, and epoxy floors built to be lived on.

## Audience

- Homeowners who want the garage to feel finished, organized, and easier to clean
- Contractors and builders who need a reliable flooring partner
- Small commercial operators with shops, storage rooms, service bays, salons, and back-of-house spaces
- Families who care about durability but still want the floor to look good

## Voice

Use a calm, capable, local voice.

- Say what will happen on the job
- Explain tradeoffs in plain language
- Use contractions when they sound natural
- Keep sentences mixed in length
- Prefer concrete details over hype
- Sound like a real operator who cares about the finish

## Avoid

- Em dashes
- Hype without proof
- Claims about warranty, insurance, certifications, years in business, or supplier systems until confirmed
- Generic marketing filler about spaces, potential, solutions, and customer paths
- Overly polished corporate language that would not sound right coming from a family business

## Visual Identity

- Base: graphite, white, warm concrete
- Accent: deep teal used sparingly
- Feel: bold contractor mark, grounded layout, tidy copy, slightly industrial finish detail
- Shapes: squared details, 8px radii or less, strong grid alignment
- Logo direction: use the dark-background logo on dark sections and the white-background logo on light sections until a transparent master file exists
- Photography direction: real garages, clean prep work, finished floors, family-safe spaces, close-up flakes, and commercial utility

## Proof Style

Until testimonials and project photos exist, proof should be framed around process and clarity:

- Written scope
- Surface preparation
- Moisture discussion
- Clean edges
- Clear cure guidance
- Job-site respect

These are credible process signals without inventing results.
